Application for Senior Product Manager, Everlab

Dear Everlab Hiring Team,

I am applying for the Senior Product Manager role. I am a founder-operator who builds AI-enabled products from discovery through to measured adoption, and I am drawn to Everlab's mission of delivering as much health, for as many people as possible, at the lowest cost. The combination of clinical depth, operational complexity and a hard cost constraint is exactly the kind of problem I want to own.

Owning products end to end is how I work. At Subject:RAW, the agency I founded, I shipped Growth OS, a demand-report engine that turns a clinic's website and location into a credible market report. I owned it from the first customer conversations and PRD through data modelling, build and deployment, then watched how clinics actually used the output and reworked the report around the jobs they were hiring it to do. I run lightweight experiments before committing engineering time, and I answer my own data questions rather than waiting on a queue.

I am comfortable across the seams between engineering, operations and clinical-style stakeholders. At AIM I consulted on e-commerce operations, migrating fulfilment from a UK-only model to a split China and UK operation and building a Live Ops dashboard with agentic alerting so the team could see problems before they cascaded. Earlier, I scaled a performance business from one site to three and past $1M in annual revenue through to acquisition, owning P&L, pricing and hiring. That experience taught me to make decisions with incomplete information and to do the unglamorous work that early-stage products need.

The healthcare context is not new to me. My background is in exercise physiology, and at Penrith Panthers I build the reporting tools that medical, performance and coaching staff rely on every day, so I am used to designing for clinicians who have no patience for noise. Growth OS already serves allied-health clinics, so I have spent real time understanding how these businesses think about patients and demand.

I would welcome the chance to talk about where I could own a domain at Everlab. Thank you for considering my application.

Sincerely,
Uday Barooah
